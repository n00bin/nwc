Core Mechanics

Rating to Percentage
Every stat has a rating value that converts to a percentage. The conversion depends on your Total Item Level (TIL).
Percentage = 50 - (TIL - Rating) / 1000 + Percent Bonuses
At 0 rating~45% base
At TIL rating50% from rating alone
Every 1,000 rating+1% to stat percentage
To reach capNeed rating + percent bonuses from gear, boons, companions, etc.
Combined Rating
Combined Rating is a single number that adds equally to ALL your core stats at once. It comes from companions, mounts, and certain gear.
Combined Rating adds its value to every core stat (Power, Defense, Crit Strike, etc.)
SourcesCompanions, mounts, insignias, companion gear
Example+2,700 CR = +2,700 to Power, Defense, Crit Strike, etc.
Why it mattersHigher CR items raise all stats, not just one

Damage Calculation

Magnitude
Every power has a magnitude number that determines how much damage or healing it does relative to your base damage. Higher magnitude = more damage.
Actual Damage = (Magnitude / 100) x Base Damage x Multipliers
Base DamageTIL / 10 (+ role bonus)
DPS Bonus+20% base damage
Healer Bonus+10% base damage
Example1,000 mag = 10x your base damage before multipliers
Damage Pipeline
Damage goes through multiple multipliers in order. Each stat multiplies the result independently.
Final Damage = Base x Power x CA x Crit x Buffs / Enemy Defense
Order of Operations
1. Base Damage(Magnitude / 100) x (TIL / 10)
2. Power Multiplierx (1 + Power %)
3. Combat Advantagex (1 + CA % - enemy Awareness %)
4. Critical StrikeIf crit: x (1 + Crit Sev % - enemy Crit Avoid %)
5. Buff/DebuffAll damage buffs/debuffs applied multiplicatively
6. Enemy Defense/ (1 + enemy Defense %)
7. Deflect CheckIf deflected: / (1 + Deflect Sev % - your Accuracy %)
Diminishing Returns
Because defensive stats use division formulas, each additional point of a stat is worth slightly less than the previous one. The stat still helps, but the benefit per point decreases.
Defense Example
0% Defense0% damage reduction
50% Defense~33% damage reduction
90% Defense~47% damage reduction
120% Defense~55% damage reduction
Damage Reduction = 1 - (1 / (1 + Defense %)). Going from 0 to 50% saves more than going from 50% to 120%.

Scaling

How Scaling Works
When you enter scaled content (older dungeons, trials), your stats are adjusted down to match the content's level. Understanding scaling is key to not losing effectiveness.
Key Rules
TIL in scaled contentDoesn't matter — stats are rescaled
What mattersStat percentages, not raw ratings
Percent bonusesMore valuable (boons, companion powers, etc.)
Utility statsAP Gain and Recharge Speed bypass scaling
The Scaling Trap
Items that give high IL but low stats (like collars or combat enchantments) can actually hurt you in scaled content. They raise your TIL without adding enough rating to match, which lowers your stat percentages after scaling.
In scaled content: prioritize stat efficiency over raw item level.

Stacking Rules

Multiplicative Stacking
Most damage buffs and debuffs in Neverwinter stack multiplicatively, not additively. This means each buff multiplies the result of the previous one.
+10% and +15% = 1.10 x 1.15 = 26.5% total (not 25%)
Damage buffsMultiplicative
Damage debuffs on enemiesMultiplicative
Defense statsMultiplicative (division formula)
Same-Source Stacking
When multiple party members use the same buff, the second instance usually provides reduced benefit.
Aura equip powers (e.g. Runic Aura)Second stack = 50% effectiveness
Spined Devil + SuccubusDo NOT stack (same debuff type)
Different buff sourcesStack fully
Consumable Stacking
One buff per category, but different categories stack with each other.
Elixir1 at a time (replaces previous)
Potion / Flask1 at a time (replaces previous)
Event Food1 at a time (replaces previous)
Stronghold Food1 at a time (replaces previous)
Invocation Blessing1 at a time (replaces previous)
Overload Enchantments1 per overload slot on gear
You can run an Elixir + Potion + Event Food + SH Food + Blessing + Overloads all at once.
