## Week of 2026-06-23 (since last publish)

### Features
- Toon Forge: new **"Group stacks Providence"** toggle (Mount → Party Buffs, on by default). The Providence mount equip power's Radiant Weapon only reaches its 8-stack cap (+16% damage) when a coordinated group all runs it; the toggle lets you model that on, or turn it off if your group isn't stacking it. Applies to both the stat panel and the optimizer.
- Toon Forge: a quiet **"add this to the Community Meta?"** nudge now appears right when you **save a build** or **copy a share link** — the moments you've decided a build is good — instead of the share option being buried in the Community menu. It's anonymous, dismissible, never blocks what you're doing, and shows again whenever you tweak and re-save (each version is its own data point); it won't pester you to re-send a build you've already shared unchanged. (Build-sharing flywheel, Report #142.)
- Toon Forge → **Community Meta**: it no longer dead-ends when your exact paragon doesn't have enough shared builds yet. If there aren't 5+ of your paragon, it now shows the meta for your **whole class + role** (every paragon) with a clear banner, instead of "not enough builds yet." Powers stay accurate because at-wills/encounters/dailies are shared across a class's paragons. (Cold-start fix for the build-sharing flywheel, Report #142.)

### Bug Fixes
- Toon Forge: two **Demonweb Empowerment** weapons were filed under a collection-tab name instead of a real slot, so they never appeared in the picker and the set couldn't complete — **Perfect Song of Lolth** (now correctly a Bard Off Hand, pairs with Perfect Nail of Lolth) and **Perfect Jewel of Lolth** (now correctly a Wizard Main Hand, pairs with Perfect Talisman of Lolth). Both screenshot-verified. (Report #147.)
- Toon Forge: fixed **Cavalry's Alarm / Cavalry's Warning** insignia bonuses being over-valued. These give "all of your ratings" for only a 20-second window after you use a Mount Combat Power, but the builder was treating that bonus as if it were always on, and the optimizer was recommending it heavily. It's now correctly marked **conditional** (it only shows in your stats under "Show Conditional", not in your standing base stats), its uptime is modeled realistically (≈25% instead of an optimistic 33%, since you can't reliably fire a mount power exactly on its 60-second cooldown), and the optimizer no longer auto-recommends it — matching what most players actually run. You can still slot it by hand if you want it.
- Toon Forge: in the optimizer's mount results, each insignia bonus now shows **what the bonus does** (its effect description) instead of repeating the list of insignias that form it (which already shows under the mount).
- Toon Forge: the **Providence** mount equip power (Brain Stealer Dragon / Swift Golden Lion) now counts toward your damage. Its "Radiant Weapon" stacks (+2% damage each, up to 8) were written only as descriptive text, so the builder credited it with **zero damage** and always favored **Ferocity** over it. It's now modeled like Ferocity, at the full **8 stacks (+16%)** — in coordinated endgame groups where everyone runs Providence, the "from any source" stacking keeps it pinned at the cap (in solo/pug play it sits lower, ~2–3 stacks; the value is tunable). PS5-verified: Providence's 2%/stack is flat at every rarity, and Ferocity correctly reads **2.1% at Celestial** (1.6% Mythic × the Celestial scaling).
- Toon Forge: **Cracked Aetherwrap** (Critical Momentum shirt) had its equip-bonus description wrong — it read *"Hitting an enemy … for 8 seconds"* when the in-game tooltip is *"**When moving in combat** … for **6 seconds**"* (the 3%-per-stack / 9% max Critical Severity values were already right, so scoring was unaffected — this was a wording fix verified against the archived tooltip).
- Toon Forge: **Bloodwoven Signs (Occult Advantage)** pants had its equip bonus backwards — it was stored as *+5% Accuracy* when the in-game tooltip is **−5% Accuracy** (the piece trades Accuracy for Combat Advantage). Screenshot-verified and corrected, so builds using this clothing now score honestly.
- Toon Forge: a database-wide sweep found **140 more weapons and accessories hidden from the pickers** — each had a rarity collection-tab name (like "Artifact Equipment") stored where its real slot should be, so it couldn't be equipped at all. Every one was checked against its in-game tooltip and moved to the correct slot: Bard lutes and dozens of class weapons to **Main/Off Hand**, plus hoods, boots, gloves, sashes and cloaks to their armor slots. They're all equippable now, and the weapon sets they belong to (Drowned, Earthen, Sunset, Vistani, Aboleth, Hellfire Engine, Legion Guard and many more) can finally complete. A handful of obscure Chultan accessories still need an in-game capture.
