## Week of 2026-06-09 (since last publish)

### Features
- **Conditional bonuses now count at honest, sustained values — everywhere.** Until now, "while above 80% HP", "stacks up to 6 times", "+9% on encounter use for 10s" style bonuses were quietly counted as if they were active 100% of the fight (only damage-type bonuses were uptime-weighted). Toon Forge now credits EVERY conditional bonus — gear, overloads, and companion procs alike — at a modeled in-combat uptime, and any line counted below 100% says so right on the panel (e.g. "~27% uptime"). Companion procs use their real trigger, chance, duration, and internal cooldown. Expect some builds to read a little lower than before — that's the honest number (one reference endgame build dropped ~7%, mostly from companion procs that were never really permanent). Two bonus families got hand-tuned uptimes after review (Charged Rejuvenation for healers at 90%, Living Magma at 55%), and a per-bonus override hook means anything misjudged can be pinned precisely. This is groundwork for the upcoming build optimizer: recommendations compare items by what they actually deliver in a fight, not their best-case tooltip.

### Features
- **Guild boons now scale with your guild's structure rank — pick it once and everything follows.** Guild boon values were locked at max-structure numbers (+3,000 stat, +800 Combined Rating each), which over-credits anyone not in a fully-built guild. The Boons step now has a structure-rank picker for each category — Offense, Defense, and Utility are three different stronghold structures that can sit at three different levels, so each gets its own rank (1–10, defaults to 10): every guild boon's stat, Combined Rating, AND item level scale with its structure's rank — the verified 300 stat (1,200 HP), 80 Combined Rating, and 100 item level per rank — and utility boon effects span their real ranges. Stat cards, the breakdown panel, Total Item Level, and the optimizer all follow the picked rank, and it's saved in builds and share links. Owner-requested — and a player report the same day (a rank-9 guild's Accuracy Bonus reading 2,700 / IL 900 in-game) confirmed the per-rank model, including that item level scales too.

### Data Additions
- **Seven missing guild boons added — including a whole stat boon nobody could pick.** Cross-checking the wiki turned up seven stronghold boons missing from our data: **Control Resist Bonus** (a full 300-per-rank Defense-category stat boon), the three **Enhance Overload** boons (PvE Slayer/Ward and Companion Ward+Slayer enhancers), **Treasure Hunter**, **Glory Bonus**, and **Healing Potion Bonus**. All seven are pickable now and correctly grant their 80 Combined Rating per structure rank; the overload-enhancement and QoL effects are described on their cards (their damage/utility side isn't simulated yet). Also fixed quietly: three utility boons (Mount Speed, Group Heal Potion, Revive Sickness) were feeding made-up stat names into the calculator's warning log — they're properly typed as effect boons now.
- **The whole Enduring enhancement family is now in-game verified at 6% — three were under-counted.** These runes' tooltips show base-quality text ("increases your X by 4%"), but owner in-game checks confirmed the player actually gains **6%** at max quality across the family. Enduring Precision (Hank the Ranger, Critical Strike) already counted 6% and just needed its card text fixed — but **Enduring Alacrity** (Diana, Movement Speed), **Enduring Craft** (Captain Elaina Sartell, Forte), and **Enduring Guard** (Eric the Cavalier, Defense) were stored at the tooltip's 4%, so builds using them were reading 2% low. All three now count 6% in Toon Forge, matching Enduring Senses (verified at 6% back in May).
- **Balgora's powers are finally complete — the last audit blocker is closed.** An owner Mount Preview screenshot filled in the long-missing **Seeing Red** equip power (40% chance on encounter powers to gain 13,125 Accuracy for 8s at Celestial, once per 15s, +3,544 Combined Rating) and confirmed **Hell's Impact** was stored at its Celestial values (Magnitude 984, DoT 151, +14.8% Incoming Damage debuff for 10s). The capture was taken at a lower rarity and scaled to the Celestial anchor with integer-perfect math — Balgora now displays correctly in the mount inspector and counts in build calculations.
- **Hellfire Engine weapon set bonus — found and structured.** This morning we stripped a made-up "+1,500 Power" from the Hellfire Engine Tow Hook; by evening the real bonus turned up on the set's own higher-tier siblings in our screenshot archive: at the start of combat you gain **+15% Stamina Regeneration and +15% Movement Speed for 10s** (refreshes on kill). All nine lower-tier Hellfire weapons (Tow Hook, Oil Stick, Instruction Manual tiers) now carry the verified bonus instead of placeholders.
- **Six more duplicate gear entries removed, two more phantom "sets" busted.** Archived tooltips settled five duplicate pairs — Blaspheme Pactblade, Perfect Mark of Lolth, Exalted Maiden's Raid Wristguards, Starhide Skullcap and Doublet, and Runefrost Hunter's Coat — each now exists exactly once with screenshot-verified stats (the deleted copies carried stray bonuses or wrong values). And the same tooltips proved "Cosmic Corsair's Armor" (12 Starweave/Starhide pieces) and "Frostforged Warplate" (36 Wintermarked/Runefrost pieces) were collection-tab names, not real sets — items don't show set lines in game, so the false set tags are gone from all 48 pieces.
- **Ultraviolet Elven Cap — the 3-vs-5-second mystery, solved.** The database had this Warlock/Wizard head twice, and the copies disagreed on how fast its Combat Advantage stack builds (every 3s vs every 5s). The archived tooltip settled it: **every 5 seconds**, 1% per stack, max 10. The correct copy (whose stacking bonus Toon Forge already counts) is now the only one — and the same tooltip revealed "Ultraviolet Armor" was another phantom collection-tab set, now cleared from all 12 pieces.

### Bug Fixes
- **The big data audit is done: every "suspicious" gear bonus in the database now has a verdict.** The final sweep put ~120 flagged bonuses in front of tooltip screenshots and community sources: the Challenger's / Survivor's / Warden's conditional families checked out exactly (1,500/2,000-rating bonuses gated on health or enemy count — all verbatim), the Underdark Ring of Brutality line got the same honest-uptime treatment as the Sudden rings (4,000 Power for 2-10s every 30s, now counted at its true 7-33%), Ring of Rising per-stack values were confirmed as real tier scaling, and the Legion Guard weapons' Power bonus was corrected from 1,500 to the sourced 1,000. Of the 505 anomalies the audit originally flagged across 6,300+ items, 300+ are now evidence-verified as real game design, 200+ data errors were fixed outright, and only ~60 stubborn cases remain — each one documented and waiting on a specific in-game screenshot.
- **Two pairs of boots were missing stats — and a whole armor family had its Combined Rating stored as zero.** The budget audit's "this item looks too weak" list paid off: Curselord's Raid and Assault Pigaches were both missing their +578 Defense (tooltip-proven, now restored), the entire Lionsmane IL-560 family had Combined Rating stored as 0 instead of 504, Lionsmane Duelist Gloves' Deflection read 148 instead of 168, and the Executioner Mask's Critical Strike read 168 instead of 126. Meanwhile 21 "too strong"-flagged families were tooltip- or community-verified as real game design — Stronghold guild rings really are that stat-dense, companion tomes follow their own budget curve, Sharandar artifacts scale differently, and old Dwarven Valley gear predates the big stat rescale — all recorded in the evidence whitelist with citations.
- **Nine more phantom "sets" busted, and two real weapon sets straightened out.** Tooltip captures confirmed the pattern again: "set" names like Hunts of Omu, Storm King's Thunder Curiosities, Rings: The Dread Sanctum and six more are collection-tab pages, not real equip sets — no in-game tooltip shows a set line for them, so the false tags are gone from 76 items. Meanwhile Living Magma and Dark Matter ARE real weapon sets, and every tooltip reads "(0/2)" — a few stray records claimed 4 pieces, now corrected to match the game. The set-completability audit also got smarter: it now knows two rings can wear the same set, that 3-piece sets like Malignant Energy legitimately complete through an Artifact, and it only raises alarms for sets whose modeled bonuses could actually be affected.
- **The classic "Sudden" rings now count at their real uptime — and four flagged item families got full evidence verdicts.** The Underdark "Ring of Sudden" line (Precision/Defense/Deflect/Lifesteal +1 to +5) grants 4,000 of a stat for 2-10 seconds out of every 30 — but the calculator was crediting that at more than half uptime. Community sources (wiki tier pages + patch history) confirmed the exact per-tier durations, and each ring now counts at its true 7-33% uptime. Meanwhile tooltip captures proved two big "suspicious" families are completely legit — League's Elite gear really does carry a +31% premium stat budget, and the Mastered Duergar 10,000-rating bonuses are real (they're HP-threshold conditionals, now honestly weighted) — and the Drowned weapons' "Heal Self 50%" turned out to be a faithful copy of the real set proc. Verified families are recorded in an evidence whitelist so the data screen stops re-flagging them.
- **Ten races had racial bonuses the calculator silently ignored — including Gith's +5% Combat Advantage.** A player noticed their Gith's Combat Advantage was reading low, and they were right: the trait text was in the database but the number behind it was never wired up — and the same was true for nine more races. Now counted: Gith's +5% Combat Advantage, Halfling's +5% Deflect and +10% Control Resist, Human's +3% Defense, Dwarf's +5% Defense, Half-Orc's +5% Critical Severity, Moon Elf's +3% Action Point Gain / +3% Stamina Regen and +10% Control Resist, Dragonborn's +3% Power / +3% Critical Strike (both variants), Metallic Dragonborn's +3% Max HP / +3% Incoming Healing, Aasimar's five-stat 2% package plus their party Healing Hands aura (counted as conditional), and Tiefling's Bloodhunt +5% damage below half health (conditional, in the damage model). Every value was verified against the wiki or the in-game trait text before wiring. Player-found. A player reported it "missing" — it wasn't, but all four database copies called it a Shirt when the Charged Precision version is actually **Pants** (their in-game report settled it; collection-tab tooltips don't show the slot, so it had been inferred). The Pants entry now carries the exact reported stats and its Charged Precision bonus (+8% Critical Strike while Stamina is above 75%) counts in the calculator at an honest uptime. A phantom "Rings, Shackles of Divinity" set tag (really a collection-tab name) was stripped from the family while in there. Player-found.
- **Snowtusk's fourth insignia slot suggested the wrong insignia.** Two player reports agreed: the universal fourth slot's suggested insignia is **Illuminated**, not Enlightened — the ghost icon was misread in the May 29 verification pass. Fixed. Player-found.
- **Master boons no longer count as permanent stats — they're proc buffs, and now they're priced like it.** Owner-caught: Deathly Rage ("chance on kill… for 10 seconds") was feeding its full +6% Power / +6% Combat Advantage / +6% Critical Severity into builds as if it were always active — and the optimizer was buying master boon ranks on that phantom value. All eight master boons now count at a modeled in-fight uptime built from their real trigger and duration: "on kill" buffs are worth almost nothing against a boss (~4%), "on encounter use" buffs sit around 49–63%, Life Lessons' below-30%-health window lands ~15%, and the two "when you heal" boons (Blessed Advantage / Blessed Resilience) only count on healer builds — zero on everyone else. Every counted line shows its uptime in the stat breakdown (e.g. "~49% uptime"), and the optimizer now spends boon points on what a boon actually delivers in a fight. And the owner read the live Deathly Rage tooltip in-game, catching two things every external source (the wiki, NW Hub, Obikin89) gets wrong: it also triggers when you take more than 50% of your max HP in a single blow, and the proc chance is an explicit 30%. Both are in the data now — Deathly Rage credits ~13% uptime instead of the ~4% a kill-only assumption gave it.
- **The four "vs one enemy family" boons no longer count as universal damage.** Cultist Power, Demonic Mastery, Dino Power, and Necrotic Mastery (+1% damage and damage resistance per point — but ONLY against Cultists / Fiends / Dinosaurs / Undead respectively) were being credited as unconditional damage, which made them look like the best boons in the tree. The general boss calculation now counts them at zero, the same policy vs-enemy-type gear bonuses already follow.
- **The optimizer was stacking Cavalry's Alarm with Cavalry's Warning — in-game, Alarm simply overwrites Warning.** The owner noticed the optimizer kept recommending both "gain X to all of your ratings for 20 seconds" mount bonuses together. Their own tooltips say they never stack (Alarm overwrites Warning), yet the calculator credited both — roughly 10,000 effective rating of phantom value that doesn't exist in the game. Toon Forge now enforces every "overwrites / does not stack with" bonus pair — Cavalry's Alarm vs Warning, Warlord's Motivation vs Inspiration, Artificer's Enticement vs Persuasion, Knight's Condemnation vs Rebuke. When both are slotted, only the stronger one counts, and the stat breakdown labels the dead one "(no effect — overwritten by ...)" so it's clear why. With the phantom value gone, the optimizer stops proposing the pairing. Slotting either bonus alone still counts at its honest ~33% uptime (a 20-second buff on a ~60-second mount power cycle). Owner-found.
- **Companion enhancements that buff two stats now count both.** A player noticed that enhancements doing multiple things were only applying one of them — and they were right. Three enhancements buff two of YOUR stats at once, but the data could only hold one: **Unflinching Will** (Bobby — Deflect AND Deflect Severity), **Impactful Maneuvers** (Minotaur — Power AND Forte), and **Exploit Weakness** (Blaspheme Assassin — Critical Strike AND Critical Severity), each up to 4.5%. Toon Forge now applies both stats, and the Companions page shows both on the enhancement card. While in there we caught **Reinvigorate** quietly feeding the calculator 9 *rating* points of Outgoing Healing (basically nothing) instead of its real **9%** — also fixed. Note: enhancements whose second effect targets your companion itself (like Counteract's companion Critical Avoidance) correctly buff only the companion, not your stat panel — those were never broken. Player-found.
- **37 duplicate gear entries resolved against the tooltip archive — and real class variants discovered.** The data screen flagged 59 same-item conflicts (two copies of one item with different stats). Tooltip screenshots adjudicated 57 of them: 37 wrong copies deleted, 8 records corrected to their screenshot-verified stats (including Astral Raider's Jackboots, the Thayan Servitor Warlock pieces, Stormforged Pactblade's Combat Advantage 500→1500, and a Bronzewood Amulet that had IL-800 stats on its IL-650 entry). Along the way we proved several "duplicates" are actually legitimate per-class variants (Prismatic and Eternal gear have class-specific stat layouts) — those were preserved, and the screen now understands class variants so it won't flag them again.
- **150 gear bonuses were secretly counting as giant percentages — a full-database sweep caught them all.** A new automated screen compared every one of the 6,369 gear entries against its own item family and found a systematic glitch from the June 8 import batches: bonuses like "7,500 Power" or "3,000 Max HP" were stored without their rating tag, so the calculator read them as *+7,500% Power* — astronomically inflating those items (Blaspheme weapons, Dragonflight Raid/Assault sets, Delzoun Sabatons and 100+ more). All 153 occurrences are fixed and the sweep also re-confirmed known open issues (like the Flayed Legion boots) on its own — it now runs as a standing data-quality gate so this class of error can't sneak in again.
- **Omen of Doom was missing 2,880 Critical Severity — and its set bonus was undervalued.** A player noticed his Critical Severity reading low. The archived in-game tooltips proved the endgame (IL 4,800) Omen of Doom carries Accuracy, Critical Strike, AND Critical Severity (2,880) — our copy was missing the third stat. The same tooltip settled the Impending Doom 2-piece: it grants a flat +7.5% Critical Severity (we had 2.5%) and +2.5% Power, plus the charge-up "Unleashed" effect (DPS +4.5% Base Damage Boost / Healer +4.5% Outgoing Healing for 20s), which the damage sim now counts at a conservative uptime. A made-up "+1% Damage Bonus" on the weapon was removed while we were in there.
- **Your summoned companion's party buff no longer double-counts.** If you summoned Tutor (+5% Combat Advantage) and also picked Tutor as a party ally, Toon Forge counted the buff twice — in the game you only ever benefit once. The calculator now skips the ally copy automatically (the slot shows a clear "not counted" warning on old builds), and the ally picker won't let you select your own summoned companion in the first place. Player-found.
- **A Warlock's full-gear sweep caught three more silent bonuses.** The Wintermarked Swiftguards' Eagle's Mastery (up to 3% Power, Critical Strike, and Critical Severity on big hits) now counts, like its higher-tier sibling already did. Maiden's Advantage — a flat 6% Combat Advantage on the Frostsilver Ring of Initiative (5% on the Coldsilver version and Eilistraee's Beauty), plus bonus Power against 2+ enemies — was tooltip-text-only on all three rings and now counts too. Biggest one: the endgame Omen of Doom and Codex of Eternal Chains weapons disagreed about which set they belong to, so their 2-piece bonus **never activated at all** — the pair is now correctly matched (Impending Doom) and the set bonus fires.
- **62 "stacks while you fight" gear bonuses now actually count.** A player noticed his Wintermarked Hunter Hood's Critical Breaker (up to 9% Critical Strike after 25 seconds in combat) wasn't showing up in Toon Forge. He was right — that bonus, and 61 others with the same "for every few seconds in combat, gain a stack" wording (on Wintermarked, Runefrost, Frostbound, Cosmic Corsair, Delzoun, Radiant Elven, Lolthian pieces and more), existed only as tooltip text the calculator skipped. All 62 are now structured so the stat panel counts them at full stacks under "Show conditional bonuses" — including multi-stat ones, the Coldsilver/Frostsilver Coil Accuracy stackers, and Wildspace-only Spelljammer's Grace. Three items (both Gladiator's Sustenance crowns and the Cuirass of the Crimson Scythe) have self-contradicting in-game tooltips and are flagged for re-capture rather than guessed at.
- **Total Item Level was reading ~1,800 high — mount collection bolster grants no item level after all.** A player's shared Warlock build showed 149,836 in Toon Forge while the game said 148,037. A full line-by-line reconstruction (using both his current and previous build links) proved the "Mount bolster IL" line added June 7 was anchored against a build whose Feet and companion-ring entries were out of date by exactly the same 1,800 the line was invented to cover. With the line removed, the build computes 148,036 vs the game's 148,037 — and the May 12 Paladin/Ranger calibrations (which matched without the line) agree. If your builds suddenly read ~1,800 lower, this is why — the game's number was right all along.
- **Audit cleanup round 2 — friendlier words everywhere.** Failed screenshot uploads on the Reports page now say "Couldn't upload your screenshot — try a smaller image or check your connection" instead of raw technical error codes, and the reports-list loading error suggests checking your connection. The Preview page's empty sections no longer show internal folder paths. Toon Forge's footer no longer flashes the placeholder text "Buff state" while loading, and the "Hide in-combat bonuses" hover tip was rewritten so checked clearly means hidden. Under the hood: a latent engine inconsistency was fixed so overload proc bonuses would be uptime-weighted exactly like gear procs if any ever appear, and the data build script now fails loudly if a file write goes wrong instead of leaving a half-written file.
