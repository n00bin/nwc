## Week of 2026-06-09 (since last publish)

### Features
- **Conditional bonuses now count at honest, sustained values — everywhere.** Until now, "while above 80% HP", "stacks up to 6 times", "+9% on encounter use for 10s" style bonuses were quietly counted as if they were active 100% of the fight (only damage-type bonuses were uptime-weighted). Toon Forge now credits EVERY conditional bonus — gear, overloads, and companion procs alike — at a modeled in-combat uptime, and any line counted below 100% says so right on the panel (e.g. "~27% uptime"). Companion procs use their real trigger, chance, duration, and internal cooldown. Expect some builds to read a little lower than before — that's the honest number (one reference endgame build dropped ~7%, mostly from companion procs that were never really permanent). Two bonus families got hand-tuned uptimes after review (Charged Rejuvenation for healers at 90%, Living Magma at 55%), and a per-bonus override hook means anything misjudged can be pinned precisely. This is groundwork for the upcoming build optimizer: recommendations compare items by what they actually deliver in a fight, not their best-case tooltip.

### Data Additions
- **Enduring Precision verified at 6%, not its tooltip's 4%.** Hank the Ranger's enhancement tooltip shows its base-quality text ("increases your Critical Strike by 4%"), but an owner in-game check confirmed the player actually gains **6%** at max quality — matching how Enduring Senses verified earlier. The site's enhancement card now reads 6%. Its three siblings (Enduring Alacrity, Craft, and Guard — currently listed at 4%) very likely scale the same way and are flagged for the same in-game check.
- **Balgora's powers are finally complete — the last audit blocker is closed.** An owner Mount Preview screenshot filled in the long-missing **Seeing Red** equip power (40% chance on encounter powers to gain 13,125 Accuracy for 8s at Celestial, once per 15s, +3,544 Combined Rating) and confirmed **Hell's Impact** was stored at its Celestial values (Magnitude 984, DoT 151, +14.8% Incoming Damage debuff for 10s). The capture was taken at a lower rarity and scaled to the Celestial anchor with integer-perfect math — Balgora now displays correctly in the mount inspector and counts in build calculations.
- **Hellfire Engine weapon set bonus — found and structured.** This morning we stripped a made-up "+1,500 Power" from the Hellfire Engine Tow Hook; by evening the real bonus turned up on the set's own higher-tier siblings in our screenshot archive: at the start of combat you gain **+15% Stamina Regeneration and +15% Movement Speed for 10s** (refreshes on kill). All nine lower-tier Hellfire weapons (Tow Hook, Oil Stick, Instruction Manual tiers) now carry the verified bonus instead of placeholders.
- **Six more duplicate gear entries removed, two more phantom "sets" busted.** Archived tooltips settled five duplicate pairs — Blaspheme Pactblade, Perfect Mark of Lolth, Exalted Maiden's Raid Wristguards, Starhide Skullcap and Doublet, and Runefrost Hunter's Coat — each now exists exactly once with screenshot-verified stats (the deleted copies carried stray bonuses or wrong values). And the same tooltips proved "Cosmic Corsair's Armor" (12 Starweave/Starhide pieces) and "Frostforged Warplate" (36 Wintermarked/Runefrost pieces) were collection-tab names, not real sets — items don't show set lines in game, so the false set tags are gone from all 48 pieces.
- **Ultraviolet Elven Cap — the 3-vs-5-second mystery, solved.** The database had this Warlock/Wizard head twice, and the copies disagreed on how fast its Combat Advantage stack builds (every 3s vs every 5s). The archived tooltip settled it: **every 5 seconds**, 1% per stack, max 10. The correct copy (whose stacking bonus Toon Forge already counts) is now the only one — and the same tooltip revealed "Ultraviolet Armor" was another phantom collection-tab set, now cleared from all 12 pieces.

### Bug Fixes
- **Companion enhancements that buff two stats now count both.** A player noticed that enhancements doing multiple things were only applying one of them — and they were right. Three enhancements buff two of YOUR stats at once, but the data could only hold one: **Unflinching Will** (Bobby — Deflect AND Deflect Severity), **Impactful Maneuvers** (Minotaur — Power AND Forte), and **Exploit Weakness** (Blaspheme Assassin — Critical Strike AND Critical Severity), each up to 4.5%. Toon Forge now applies both stats, and the Companions page shows both on the enhancement card. While in there we caught **Reinvigorate** quietly feeding the calculator 9 *rating* points of Outgoing Healing (basically nothing) instead of its real **9%** — also fixed. Note: enhancements whose second effect targets your companion itself (like Counteract's companion Critical Avoidance) correctly buff only the companion, not your stat panel — those were never broken. Player-found.
- **37 duplicate gear entries resolved against the tooltip archive — and real class variants discovered.** The data screen flagged 59 same-item conflicts (two copies of one item with different stats). Tooltip screenshots adjudicated 57 of them: 37 wrong copies deleted, 8 records corrected to their screenshot-verified stats (including Astral Raider's Jackboots, the Thayan Servitor Warlock pieces, Stormforged Pactblade's Combat Advantage 500→1500, and a Bronzewood Amulet that had IL-800 stats on its IL-650 entry). Along the way we proved several "duplicates" are actually legitimate per-class variants (Prismatic and Eternal gear have class-specific stat layouts) — those were preserved, and the screen now understands class variants so it won't flag them again.
- **150 gear bonuses were secretly counting as giant percentages — a full-database sweep caught them all.** A new automated screen compared every one of the 6,369 gear entries against its own item family and found a systematic glitch from the June 8 import batches: bonuses like "7,500 Power" or "3,000 Max HP" were stored without their rating tag, so the calculator read them as *+7,500% Power* — astronomically inflating those items (Blaspheme weapons, Dragonflight Raid/Assault sets, Delzoun Sabatons and 100+ more). All 153 occurrences are fixed and the sweep also re-confirmed known open issues (like the Flayed Legion boots) on its own — it now runs as a standing data-quality gate so this class of error can't sneak in again.
- **Omen of Doom was missing 2,880 Critical Severity — and its set bonus was undervalued.** A player noticed his Critical Severity reading low. The archived in-game tooltips proved the endgame (IL 4,800) Omen of Doom carries Accuracy, Critical Strike, AND Critical Severity (2,880) — our copy was missing the third stat. The same tooltip settled the Impending Doom 2-piece: it grants a flat +7.5% Critical Severity (we had 2.5%) and +2.5% Power, plus the charge-up "Unleashed" effect (DPS +4.5% Base Damage Boost / Healer +4.5% Outgoing Healing for 20s), which the damage sim now counts at a conservative uptime. A made-up "+1% Damage Bonus" on the weapon was removed while we were in there.
- **Your summoned companion's party buff no longer double-counts.** If you summoned Tutor (+5% Combat Advantage) and also picked Tutor as a party ally, Toon Forge counted the buff twice — in the game you only ever benefit once. The calculator now skips the ally copy automatically (the slot shows a clear "not counted" warning on old builds), and the ally picker won't let you select your own summoned companion in the first place. Player-found.
- **A Warlock's full-gear sweep caught three more silent bonuses.** The Wintermarked Swiftguards' Eagle's Mastery (up to 3% Power, Critical Strike, and Critical Severity on big hits) now counts, like its higher-tier sibling already did. Maiden's Advantage — a flat 6% Combat Advantage on the Frostsilver Ring of Initiative (5% on the Coldsilver version and Eilistraee's Beauty), plus bonus Power against 2+ enemies — was tooltip-text-only on all three rings and now counts too. Biggest one: the endgame Omen of Doom and Codex of Eternal Chains weapons disagreed about which set they belong to, so their 2-piece bonus **never activated at all** — the pair is now correctly matched (Impending Doom) and the set bonus fires.
- **62 "stacks while you fight" gear bonuses now actually count.** A player noticed his Wintermarked Hunter Hood's Critical Breaker (up to 9% Critical Strike after 25 seconds in combat) wasn't showing up in Toon Forge. He was right — that bonus, and 61 others with the same "for every few seconds in combat, gain a stack" wording (on Wintermarked, Runefrost, Frostbound, Cosmic Corsair, Delzoun, Radiant Elven, Lolthian pieces and more), existed only as tooltip text the calculator skipped. All 62 are now structured so the stat panel counts them at full stacks under "Show conditional bonuses" — including multi-stat ones, the Coldsilver/Frostsilver Coil Accuracy stackers, and Wildspace-only Spelljammer's Grace. Three items (both Gladiator's Sustenance crowns and the Cuirass of the Crimson Scythe) have self-contradicting in-game tooltips and are flagged for re-capture rather than guessed at.
- **Total Item Level was reading ~1,800 high — mount collection bolster grants no item level after all.** A player's shared Warlock build showed 149,836 in Toon Forge while the game said 148,037. A full line-by-line reconstruction (using both his current and previous build links) proved the "Mount bolster IL" line added June 7 was anchored against a build whose Feet and companion-ring entries were out of date by exactly the same 1,800 the line was invented to cover. With the line removed, the build computes 148,036 vs the game's 148,037 — and the May 12 Paladin/Ranger calibrations (which matched without the line) agree. If your builds suddenly read ~1,800 lower, this is why — the game's number was right all along. — friendlier words everywhere.** Failed screenshot uploads on the Reports page now say "Couldn't upload your screenshot — try a smaller image or check your connection" instead of raw technical error codes, and the reports-list loading error suggests checking your connection. The Preview page's empty sections no longer show internal folder paths. Toon Forge's footer no longer flashes the placeholder text "Buff state" while loading, and the "Hide in-combat bonuses" hover tip was rewritten so checked clearly means hidden. Under the hood: a latent engine inconsistency was fixed so overload proc bonuses would be uptime-weighted exactly like gear procs if any ever appear, and the data build script now fails loudly if a file write goes wrong instead of leaving a half-written file.
